What is a Hackathon?

Definition

A hackathon is an event where programmers, designers, and innovators collaborate intensively on software or hardware projects, typically over 24-72 hours.

Duration

Most hackathons last between 24 to 48 hours, providing teams with focused time to brainstorm, develop, and present their ideas.

Who Can Participate

Open to students, professionals, and enthusiasts of all skill levels. No prior experience required, just passion and creativity!

Goals

Build innovative solutions, learn new technologies, network with peers, and showcase your skills to potential employers.

Hackathon Process

1

Registration

Sign up for the event, create a team or join an existing one, and prepare your development environment.

2

Kickoff & Theme Announcement

Event opens with an opening ceremony, introduction, rules explanation, and problem statement or theme reveal.

3

Ideation & Planning

Brainstorm ideas, define project scope, allocate tasks, and outline your approach to building the solution.

4

Development

Code, design, and build your project. Mentors and sponsors provide support, APIs, and resources.

5

Submission & Demo

Submit your project, create a demo video or live presentation showcasing your solution.

6

Judging & Awards

Judges evaluate projects based on innovation, functionality, and impact. Winners are announced and prizes awarded.
